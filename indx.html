story of BMW

BMW, short for Bayerische Motoren Werke AG (Bavarian Motor Works), is a German company that began in 1916. Initially, it focused on aircraft engines, especially during World War I, due to Germany,s growing aviation needs. After the war, restrictions on aircraft manufacturing led BMW to shift focus. By 1923, they had entered the motorcycle industry, creating the R32, BMW is first motorcycle, which helped them build their reputation for engineering.




BMW ventured into cars in 1928, acquiring a small car company and releasing its first car, the BMW 3/15. The company's focus on performance and engineering innovation grew, though it struggled financially during and after World War II. In the 1950s, BMW found stability with the Isetta, a small, bubble-shaped car that revived its finances, followed by luxury cars that appealed to affluent customers.




In the 1960s, BMW firmly established itself as a luxury brand, creating the "New Class" series, a line of sport sedans that defined the BMW identity. This set the stage for popular series like the 3, 5, and 7 Series, which continue to be flagship models. By the 1990s, BMW expanded, acquiring brands like Rolls-Royce and MINI, which reinforced its luxury market position.




Today, BMW is celebrated globally for luxury, innovation, and performance, actively expanding into electric and autonomous vehicles, keeping its focus on cutting-edge engineering while remaining true to its sporty, high-performance roots.
